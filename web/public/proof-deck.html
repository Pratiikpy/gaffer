Prediction Markets & Settlement

The result releases the money — nobody posts it.

Most on-chain markets settle by an operator posting the result and the contract trusting them. GAFFER proves the result to the chain instead. This is the exact settlement primitive the track asks builders to write.

A CPI into validate_stat

The LATCH kernel's settle fires a Cross-Program Invocation into TxLINE's on-chain validate_stat, which re-verifies the signed Merkle proof against the daily_scores_roots anchored on the oracle program, and returns a bool. The kernel reads it via return-data and pays only on true — or void-refunds if the predicate was never proven.

The settler's only power is to ask, and to pay the fee for asking. Cranking is permissionless: a bad crank just wastes its own fee. It can never misdirect a payout.

Trading Tools & Agents

The Gaffer's Ear — reading the match from the market.

An autonomous agent that infers real match events from the de-margined odds line alone, and anchors each call on-chain with a timestamp that can't be back-dated. It calls the goal before the score feed finalises. A supervisor keeps a fleet of six per-match agents plus a settlement keeper pointed at whatever the tournament is playing — no human in the loop.

Deterministic, self-tested, on-chain

A pure classifier (agents/ear.mjs): a large one-sided implied-probability jump — absolute or relative, so a favourite extending its lead isn't missed — is a goal; sustained market silence is a stoppage; full time is anchored only on the feed's positive finalisation, never on silence, so a feed outage can't write a false result.

Every call is committed on-chain the instant it's made, with an Explorer receipt. Self-test: 11/11. And it grades itself: on Argentina 3–1 Switzerland the Ear went 4/5 goal calls confirmed — 80% against the signed final score, exposed as a live signal feed at /api/ear-record.

# reproduce the Ear's logic gate
node agents/ear.mjs --selftest
  ✓ favourite goal caught by relative jump
  ✓ near-certain twitch is not a goal
  selftest: PASS 11/11

The fleet, running unattended

One supervisor (agents/worker.mjs) discovers fixtures from the schedule, runs one isolated child per match (detector, market-maker, CLV tracker, arena, the Read explainer, the Ear), restarts a crash, and settles by poking the keeper the moment a match ends. A dedicated 20s keeper sweeps the live slate.

Deployed under systemd on a DigitalOcean droplet, keyless, with every decision appended to JSONL logs as kept evidence.

Honest boundaries

The limits, next to the green numbers.

Devnet only. All real-money settlement runs on Solana devnet; nothing here has economic value yet, and none of these guarantees have run under mainnet adversarial conditions.
Odds are live; scores are post-match. The dev feed streams in-running odds to the second but not live score events, so pool settlement resolves once the match finalises. "Reacts live" is the market and the Ear — never a fabricated scoreline. The app shows "score not in yet", never a fake 0-0.
The Ear's real-match capture is next. Its logic is deterministic and self-tested and the on-chain commit path is real; a full capture on a genuine live World Cup match is the next proof, and the tournament is the only window to film it.
No demand data. Testnet only — the market is argued from problem shape and a shipped product, not from users, retention, or revenue.
